# Observational Facilities

Our research group extensively utilizes state-of-the-art national facilities located at **Devasthal Observatory** (ARIES), near Nainital, India, at an altitude of 2450 meters. This location provides excellent astronomical seeing, low background light levels, and clear nights.

---

## 1. 3.6m Devasthal Optical Telescope (DOT)

The **3.6m Devasthal Optical Telescope** is India's largest steerable optical and near-infrared telescope. Commissioned in 2016, it is a major national facility for extragalactic and galactic studies.

*   **Key Features:** Ritchey-Chrétien design with active primary mirror optics.
*   **Instruments Used:**
    *   **ADFOSC:** ARIES Devasthal Faint Object Spectrograph and Camera, used for deep multi-band optical imaging and low-to-medium resolution spectroscopy of GRB afterglows and supernovae.
    *   **TIRCAM2:** Near-infrared camera for J, H, K band imaging.
    *   **HESP:** High-Resolution Echelle Spectrograph, providing high-resolution spectroscopy.
*   **Role in our Group:** Rapid spectroscopy to classify young supernovae, monitoring long-term afterglow decays, and conducting deep host-galaxy surveys.

---

## 2. 4.0m International Liquid Mirror Telescope (ILMT)

The **4.0m International Liquid Mirror Telescope (ILMT)** is the first liquid mirror telescope in India and the largest in Asia.

*   **Key Features:** It uses a rotating container of liquid mercury to form a paraboloidal primary mirror. It operates in a fixed zenith-pointing mode.
*   **Operation:** Utilizing a large-format CCD camera in drift-scan mode, it continuously scans a 27-arcminute wide strip of the sky every night.
*   **Role in our Group:** Continuous survey monitoring in the g, r, and i bands to automatically detect transient objects like supernovae, variable stars, and asteroids, feeding into our automated transient pipelines.

---

## 3. 1.3m Devasthal Fast Optical Telescope (DFOT)

The **1.3m Devasthal Fast Optical Telescope** is a wide-field optical telescope designed for fast pointing and high-cadence monitoring.

*   **Key Features:** Equipped with a 2k x 2k CCD camera and a wide field of view.
*   **Role in our Group:** Rapid follow-up of transient triggers. It is the primary workhorse for early-phase optical photometry of Gamma-Ray Burst afterglows, stellar flares, and active galactic nuclei (AGN).

---

## 4. Space-Based & International Observatories

To supplement ground-based data, our group routinely requests and analyzes data from high-energy space missions and international facilities:
*   **AstroSat (ISRO):** India's first dedicated multi-wavelength space observatory (UV, Soft X-ray, Hard X-ray).
*   **Swift & Fermi (NASA):** Essential for prompt gamma-ray detection, localization, and early X-ray afterglow data.
*   **Hubble Space Telescope (HST) & JWST:** Used for late-time, deep imaging of host galaxies and faint supernova remnants.
