### 1. Indian Researchers Lead Study on Supernova SN 2023zcu to Refine Cosmic Distance Measurements (June 2026)
An international team led by **Monalisa Dubey**, **Dr. Kuntal Misra**, and **Naveen Dukiya** from ARIES has published a detailed photometric and spectroscopic analysis of the Type IIP supernova **SN 2023zcu** in the spiral galaxy **NGC 2139**. 

By applying the Expanding Photospheric Method (EPM), the researchers successfully determined the supernova's distance (90.7 million light-years) and estimated its progenitor's birth mass to be about 12 times that of our Sun. This high-impact study, published in *The Astrophysical Journal*, marks a significant step forward in calibrating cosmic distance indicators and understanding the physics of core-collapse events.

*   **Original Publication:** [The Astrophysical Journal](https://doi.org/10.3847/1538-4357/ae3c7e)

### 2. First Light Achieved for the 4m International Liquid Mirror Telescope (ILMT) at Devasthal (June 2022)
The **4m International Liquid Mirror Telescope (ILMT)** at the ARIES Devasthal Observatory has successfully achieved first light, commissioning India's first liquid-mirror observatory and the largest zenith-pointing telescope of its kind in the world. 

Using a spinning pool of liquid mercury to form a perfect parabolic reflecting surface, the telescope scans a narrow strip of the sky daily as the Earth rotates. **Dr. Kuntal Misra**, Project Investigator for the ILMT at ARIES, highlighted the telescope's unique engineering and its enormous potential for high-cadence time-domain surveys, which will discover thousands of supernovae, gravitational-wave counterparts, and space debris.
